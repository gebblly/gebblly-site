ACCESSIBILITY STATEMENT GEBBLLY Platform | English Translation in Israeli-Law Legal Style Last updated: 15 March 2026 Important Notice. This English version is a formal translation and presentation of the Hebrew accessibility statement supplied by the platform operator. It is drafted in a clear legal-document format and uses terminology aligned with Israeli-law practice. 1. Scope of the Statement This Accessibility Statement is issued by Adam Salalha, ID / business number 324930510, as the operator of the GEBBLLY platform (the "Operator" or the "Platform"). This Statement applies to the digital services of the Platform, including the following: • The website: www.gebblly.co.il • The customer application: GEBBLLY CUSTOMER • The merchant application: GEBBLLY RESTAURANT • The courier application: GEBBLLY DRIVER The Operator attributes importance to providing an accessible, equal, respectful, and reasonable digital service to all users, including persons with disabilities, and acts, to the extent possible and subject to technological, operational, and programmatic limitations, to promote the accessibility of the Platform’s digital services and to improve them from time to time. 2. Legal Framework and Scope of Accessibility Adjustments The Platform is developed, maintained, and reviewed, to the extent possible, in accordance with the applicable laws of the State of Israel, including the Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Regulations (Service Accessibility Adjustments), 5773-2013, and with reference to the recommendations of Israeli Standard SI 5568 at AA level. Without derogating from the generality of the foregoing, reasonable efforts are made to implement, inter alia, the following accessibility adjustments: • Keyboard operation, where relevant to the particular interface; • Zoom capability up to 200% without material loss of information or functionality; • Compatibility with commonly used screen readers; • Adaptation for use in common browsers and on mobile devices; • Improvement of heading structures, navigation elements, component labelling, and forms, to the extent applicable within the service interfaces. 3. Compatibility with Assistive Technologies Accessibility reviews and adjustments are carried out, to the extent possible, with reference to common assistive technologies, including: • VoiceOver; • TalkBack; • NVDA; • Magnification tools and built-in accessibility functions of common operating systems and browsers. 4. Accessibility Status and Known Limitations As of the publication date of this Statement, the accessibility of the Platform is subject to ongoing review, maintenance, and improvement. Accordingly, certain parts of the website or applications may not yet be fully accessible, or not all required adjustments may have been fully completed. Without derogating from the foregoing, accessibility limitations may exist, inter alia, in relation to: • Third-party components, content, or services; • External integrations that are not under the Operator’s full control; • Areas that are undergoing development, updating, maintenance, or ongoing upgrade. The Operator continues to act reasonably and on an ongoing basis to reduce accessibility gaps and to remedy deficiencies identified from time to time. 5. Method of Review Accessibility aspects are reviewed from time to time by means of internal checks, operational testing, and ongoing monitoring, including examination of central components of the website and applications. 6. Accessibility Requests and Inquiries Any person who encounters an accessibility difficulty, an operational issue affecting accessibility, or a need to receive information, a document, or a service in an accessible format, may contact the Operator. The Operator shall make reasonable efforts to review the inquiry and address it within a reasonable time and in accordance with the circumstances of the matter. For the purpose of efficient handling, it is recommended that the inquiry include, to the extent possible: • A description of the issue; • The action you attempted to perform; • A link to the relevant page or screen, if available; • The type of device, browser, or operating system used. 7. Public Reception and Physical Accessibility Arrangements The Platform operator does not maintain a permanent in-person public reception, and its activity is carried out through remote digital services. Accordingly, as of the date of this Statement, the Platform does not operate a permanent in-person service point for the public. 9. Updates to the Statement The Operator reserves the right to update this Accessibility Statement from time to time, in accordance with changes in the services, applicable legal requirements, technological developments, or any other operational need. 10. Governing Interpretation This document is intended to reflect the accessibility commitments of the Platform under applicable Israeli law. To the extent any inconsistency arises between this English version and the controlling Hebrew source text, the Hebrew version shall prevail, subject always to mandatory provisions of applicable law. End of Statement
